About CEEW

The Council on Energy, Environment and Water is one of Asia's leading not-for-profit policy research institutions. CEEW uses data, integrated analysis, and strategic outreach to explain – and change – the use, reuse, and misuse of resources.

How we developed the Model ?

Identify & Conceptualize

Define research questions on Indian bovine breeds. Build interdisciplinary teams for comprehensive breed classification and analysis.

Understand & Analyze

Collect and analyze data on 32 bovine breeds, including 26 indigenous and 6 exotic breeds, utilizing 4850 data points post-augmentation.

Convene & Communicate

Present findings on breed classification, including data augmentation techniques and model performance metrics, to stakeholders in agriculture and animal husbandry.

Support & Implement

Implement YOLOv8.2.1 model for bovine breed classification, achieving 78-80% accuracy. Support decision-making in biodiversity conservation and livestock management.

Reflect & Learn

Evaluate model performance, analyzing class-wise precision, recall, and mean Average Precision. Identify areas for improvement in breed classification accuracy.

Evolve & Innovate

Continuously refine the model through fine-tuning, dataset diversification, and exploration of advanced techniques to enhance breed classification accuracy and applicability.
